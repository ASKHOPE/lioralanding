Cookie Policy

Last Updated: April 9, 2026  |  Effective: April 9, 2026

Straightforward cookie use. We use cookies to make the platform work properly and to understand how it's being used. This policy explains what cookies are, which ones we use, and how you can control them.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They allow us to remember your preferences, keep you logged in, and analyze how the platform is used. Similar technologies include local storage, session storage, and pixel tags — this policy applies to all of them.

2. Cookies We Use

We categorize our cookies as follows:

Category Purpose Duration Can be Disabled?
Strictly Necessary Session management, authentication, CSRF protection. The platform cannot function without these. Session / 30 days No (required)
Functional Remember your preferences (theme, timezone, last viewed dashboard). Not required but improves experience. Up to 1 year Yes
Analytics Help us understand aggregate usage patterns (pages visited, session duration, feature adoption). All data is anonymized. Up to 2 years Yes
Marketing Measure the effectiveness of campaigns and allow retargeting (only on the marketing website, not inside the app). Up to 2 years Yes

3. Specific Cookies in Use

Cookie Name Type Description
lm_session Strictly Necessary Stores your encrypted session token to keep you logged in.
lm_csrf Strictly Necessary Protects form submissions against Cross-Site Request Forgery attacks.
lm_prefs Functional Stores dashboard layout preferences and notification settings.
_ga, _gid Analytics Google Analytics cookies for aggregate traffic analysis (IP anonymization is enabled).
_fbp Marketing Facebook Pixel — used only on the marketing landing page to measure ad effectiveness.

4. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies on our platform are placed by third parties:

  • Google Analytics — for anonymized usage analytics. Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Stripe — for secure payment processing. Stripe's Privacy Policy.
  • Facebook/Meta Pixel — for marketing campaign measurement (landing page only). Meta's Privacy Policy.

We do not control third-party cookies. We encourage you to review their respective privacy policies.

5. How to Control Cookies

You have several options to control or delete cookies:

  • Cookie Consent Banner: When you first visit, you can choose which cookie categories to accept or reject.
  • Browser Settings: Most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies. Note: Blocking strictly necessary cookies will prevent you from logging in.
  • Opt-Out Links:
    • Google Analytics: opt out here
    • Facebook: Manage preferences in your Facebook Ad Preferences
  • Do Not Track: We respect browser-level "Do Not Track" signals for analytics cookies.

6. Changes to This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy when we add new features or change our data practices. Material changes will be signalled via the cookie consent banner on your next visit.
